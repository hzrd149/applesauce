<objective>
Close the four WARNING-severity anti-patterns from 12.3-VERIFICATION.md that
were left open by the shipped implementation: monotonic auth-driver
registration (WR-04), undisposed extras holders on the client and invite
manager (WR-05), a restartable invite watcher with a one-way-disposed holder
(WR-06), and the repeated `transport()` recomputation in
`private-channel.openLive()` (WR-07). Also perform a truth pass over the five
engine JSDoc claims about extras behavior, now that plan 08 restored literal
byte-identity for the no-extras configuration.

Purpose: each of these leaves a live resource (a NIP-42 driver answering
challenges from a de-configured relay, a subscription pinning the app's extras
source hot, a frozen extras snapshot) attached to state the app believes it
released. None leaks extras into protocol state, so none is a blocker — but all
four are concrete lifecycle defects introduced by this phase's diff.

Output: a per-URL auth driver registry, a consistent stop()-is-pause /
dispose()-releases lifecycle rule across all five engines, a restart-safe
watcher, and JSDoc that matches the code.
</objective>
