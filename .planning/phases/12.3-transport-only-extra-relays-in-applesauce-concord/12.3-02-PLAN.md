<objective>
Give `ConcordPrivateChannel` the transport-only extras treatment: accept the
pass-through `extraRelays` option (D-13), hold one `ExtraRelays` snapshot for the
whole engine (D-04), and union the extras into every transport surface this engine
owns â€” `ensureAuth` (D-03), the `ChannelSyncContext.relays` handed to channel sync,
and the live gift-wrap subscription â€” while re-deriving `connected$`/`authenticated$`
reactively (D-07/D-08) and preserving the existing author-signature churn guard in
its reactive form (D-09).

Per Claude's Discretion in CONTEXT.md, this engine DOES receive the same merged
treatment as the community's status observables: consistency argues yes, and a
private channel dialling a cache relay while reporting connection health against a
different set would be incoherent.

This engine is a pure transport consumer â€” it builds no invite links, no bundles,
and no `material.relays` â€” so merging inside it is unconditionally safe. It runs in
wave 2 (before `community.ts`) because `community.ts` will pass `extraRelays` into
this engine's options bag and needs the field to exist first.

Purpose: one of the four engines whose sockets must follow the app's cache relay.
Output: `ConcordPrivateChannelOptions.extraRelays` plus a private `transport()`.

No changeset (D-15).
