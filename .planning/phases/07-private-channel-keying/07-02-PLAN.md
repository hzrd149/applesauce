<objective>
Close CHAN-06: give clients a reactive, first-class affordance to distinguish a visible-but-inaccessible private channel from one they hold a key for, without hand-rolling a `material.channels` lookup. `channels$` becomes a `ChannelView[]` stream carrying `accessible: boolean`, and — critically — it must react to out-of-band key grants/revocations that mutate `this.keys.material` with NO simultaneous control-plane fold (the reactivity gap RESEARCH.md surfaced, not in CONTEXT.md).

Purpose: The exact Accordian scenario — a Direct Invite grant landing with no metadata edition change — must flip `accessible` reactively so composer/invite enable-disable works. `accessible` is client-local state; it rides the emitted view, never the folded `ChannelMetadata`.
Output: The `ChannelView` type, the `materialChanged$` plumbing, the redefined `channels$`, and a reactivity test that grants a key with no other community activity.
</objective>
