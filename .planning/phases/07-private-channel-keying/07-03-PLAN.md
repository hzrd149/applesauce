<objective>
Close CHAN-02 and the client-level tail of CHAN-05/ROTATE-03/TEST-02. With Plan 01, a keyless private channel has no `keys.channels` entry, so `planeKeyFor` would throw the generic `unknown channel` on send — indistinguishable from a truly unknown id. The client has the channel state + key-holding info at send time, so `sendMessage`/`sendEvent` guard earlier and throw an exported, `instanceof`-catchable `MissingChannelKeyError` — the exact distinction the Accordian composer bug needed. This plan also closes the two client-level test gaps: TEST-02 case 5 (grant flow round-trip) and the ROTATE-03 in-session rekey path (rotate -> send -> verify new plane).

Purpose: Give consumers a precise, typed send-rejection to disable the composer, and prove the rekey takes effect immediately at the level H08's symptom actually manifested (a subsequent send).
Output: `MissingChannelKeyError`, the `sendMessage`/`sendEvent` guard, and three client-level tests.
</objective>
